## About Me

Born after 2005, Gen Z, sophomore majoring in Electronic Information Engineering. I play basketball, game (LoL, CS2), and I'm learning English on the side — not great at it yet, but working on it.

I'm a science student, so I'm probably not great at writing articles. I think you should make a sentence, a complex concept, simpler — not obscure and hard to follow. So I keep my articles simple and concise (which doesn't mean low on information).

## What This Blog Is For

Written for myself. To record things I've figured out, and to verify whether I've actually figured them out. If you can follow along too, great.

## About This Site

No ads, and there won't be. I can guarantee that. I hate ads, and I don't want anyone here to be interrupted — including me, because I'm a reader here too.

I care a lot about the experience. The site should be fast, the pages should be clean, and once you open it you shouldn't have to think much — just find what you want to read.

As for making money, I'll figure that out later. But there's only one condition: not at the cost of the experience.

## How This Site Was Built

I'll go into the tech stack in detail in another article. The short version: I write a little of the code myself, but most of it I hand off to Claude Code to build this site for me. Claude Code is really awesome — apart from the fact that Anthropic, the company, is a tiny bit anti-China (though Claude is currently the foreign large model with the best Chinese support), it has basically no downsides. **A note:** I don't care where this company sits politically. I only care about one thing — is the product good to use? If it's good, I use it. If it's not, then even if your political stance leaned toward China, I still wouldn't use your product.
